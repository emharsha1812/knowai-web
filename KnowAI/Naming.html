§ NAMING · ROUND 02 · invented + uncommon

Names that aren't taken.

Common dictionary words — Marginalia, Atelier, Codex, Primer — are mostly gone. This round leans on three strategies that produce defensible names: coined words (no existing meaning to fight), uncommon compounds (two short words that don't already collide), and specific Greek/Latin roots (ownable in the ML category).
FILE · naming-round-02
8 CONCEPTS
CoinedMade-up words. Zero baggage but need a story.
CompoundTwo short words. Vivid, easy to remember.
Greek/LatinReal roots, no English collision yet.
Avoid"AI" suffix. "-ly" SaaS endings. Anything Codex / Primer / Lyceum-adjacent.
On availability: I've biased toward names that look clear, but you must verify before committing — domain registrar, USPTO trademark search (and your jurisdiction equivalents), and a quick GitHub/social handle check. Treat these as design candidates, not legal clearance.
01 · COMPOUND workshop · editorial
Kernfolio/KURN·foh·lee·oh/
kern (the kernel — the small hard core inside something) + folio (a leaf of a book). The hard part of the paper, on the page.
"A working library of ML, one folio per kernel."
DOMAINkernfolio.com
kernfolio.dev
TONEquietly nerdy — typographic & ML wink
RISKtwo-word coinage, takes a beat to read first time

Nóema/NOH·ə·mə/
n. (Greek, νόημα) the thought — what is thought, as distinct from the act of thinking. Used in phenomenology for the content of a mental act.
"The thing you're trying to know, on the page."

noema.ml
TONEintellectual, considered, European
RISKNoema Magazine exists (different category) — coexistence likely fine
04 · LATIN ROOT marginal · annotation
Scholia/SKOH·lee·ə/
n. pl. (Greek/Latin) ancient marginal notes by scholars on classical texts — explanatory glosses, alternate readings, arguments with the author. Plural of scholion.
"Read the canon. Then write the scholia."
DOMAINscholia.dev
scholia.school
TONEold library, learned, plural-of-scholarship
RISKScholia.toolforge.org exists (Wikidata project, different) — distinct domain ok

n. Old Norse / Old English compound metaphors — "whale-road" for sea, "battle-sweat" for blood. A way of knowing a thing by describing what it does.
"Every concept has a kenning. Find it."

kennings.school
